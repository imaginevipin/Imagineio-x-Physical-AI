The Problem

Training data is the bottleneck.

schedule

Asset prep takes weeks

40h of rigging per kitchen. 2–4 weeks of cleanup per batch. Teams spend more time fixing assets than training models.

precision_manufacturing

Physics properties don't come for free

Joints, limits, and constraints have to be authored manually — they don't come from geometry. Every scene change means another round of script fixes.

label_off

Raw 3D has no structure

Semantic labels, collision meshes, and UV integrity don't come with geometry. ML engineers end up debugging UV maps instead of training perception models.

moving

Scale takes months, not days

Going from a handful of scenes to training volume requires months of manual work. Every new product or environment starts from zero — there's no reuse.

How It's Built

The structured model behind every environment we generate.

Each product is stored as a rich data model — not just geometry. Dimensions, materials, joint limits, articulation rules, and semantic labels are all captured once and reused across every scene, variant, and export.

How It Works

From structured product data to training-ready environments.

01

Every object is labeled at creation — no post-processing required

Every object in a scene carries its semantic class, instance ID, and physical dimensions from the moment it's generated — not added after rendering. Labels like dishwasher, base_cabinet, and countertop are structural outputs of the product graph, not a post-processing step you have to run.

02

One base environment, multiplied into thousands of valid training scenes

Materials, lighting, layout, and object placement are all parameter-driven — so every variant is structurally valid by construction. 1 base environment × 9 material finishes × 4 lighting rigs × 3 layout arrangements = 432 environments. Add clutter and spatial randomization and you're generating tens of thousands of training-ready scenes.

03

Train on environments grounded in the real world

Surfaces use PBR materials derived from real product specs. Layouts are dimensionally accurate. Every object is semantically labeled. Your models train on grounded environments — not procedural approximations, not AI-hallucinated scenes.
