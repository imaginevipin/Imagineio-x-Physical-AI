The Problem

Training data is the bottleneck.

01 · Asset Operations

Asset prep takes weeks

40h of rigging per kitchen. 2–4 weeks of cleanup per batch. Teams spend more time fixing assets than training models.

02 · Physics Authoring

Physics can't be improvised

Joints, limits, and constraints have to be authored manually — they don't come from geometry. Every scene change means another round of script fixes.

03 · Semantics

Raw 3D has no structure

Semantic labels, collision meshes, and UV integrity don't come with geometry. ML engineers end up debugging UV maps instead of training perception models.

04 · Throughput

Scale takes months

Going from a handful of scenes to training volume requires months of manual work. Every new product or environment starts from zero — there's no reuse.

How It's Built

The structured model behind every environment we generate.

Each product is stored as a rich data model — not just geometry. Dimensions, materials, joint limits, articulation rules, and semantic labels are all captured once and reused across every scene, variant, and export.

How It Works

From structured product data to training-ready environments.

A controlled pipeline turns structured inputs into environments that are labeled, validated, and ready for simulation.

01

Ingest structured source data

Product graph, materials, dimensions, and metadata enter the pipeline as the source of truth.

02

Normalize semantics

Classes, hierarchy, and object identity are standardized before anything is rendered or exported.

03

Validate physics and variation

Collision, articulation, and scene permutations are checked so outputs stay structurally valid.

04

Deploy simulation-ready output

Approved environments move into OpenUSD and downstream simulation workflows without manual cleanup.
